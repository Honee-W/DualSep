DPCRN: An Effective Light-weight Solution for In-car Speech Separation

Ziqian Wang, Jiayao Sun, Xingchen Li, Lei Xie
Audio, Speech and Language Processing Group (ASLP@NPU) School of Computer Science, Northwestern Polytechnical University, Xi‚Äôan, China
Paper arXiv

Abstract

The rise of deep learning and advancements in voice-activated technologies have propelled the development of intelligent in-car communications. However, the challenge of efficiently eliminating interference and separating speech in a complex, multi-talker in-car environment with resource and time limitations persists, which is crucial for downstream tasks like speech recognition and semantic understanding. This paper presents a lightweight framework incorporating digital signal processing and neural networks, utilizing dual encoders to effectively leverage spatial prior. Our proposed system achieves a unified approach for both streaming and non-streaming scenarios. With 0.83M model parameters and 0.39 real-time factor on Intel Core i7 (2.6GHz) CPU, our proposed framework outperforms robust baseline models across various metrics, achieving effective speech separation in distinct speech zones.
